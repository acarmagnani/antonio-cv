That project later served as the material foundation for the school's installation at the Venice Architecture Biennale 2025. Working through whole-life carbon from a materials perspective gave me a grounding in LCA methodology that I want to apply at a larger and more varied scale.

What made that project useful was not just the LCA framework but the ability to integrate quantitative data into actionable decisions, and that same combination has defined my consulting work. At Ramboll, I supported double materiality assessments aligned with CSRD financial materiality standards, producing structured analytical outputs for client-facing deliverables. At Sweco, I contributed to climate and environmental methodology documents for internationally funded infrastructure projects, working across multidisciplinary teams and preparing technical background materials for tenders and proposals. In both contexts, the central task was the same: take complex environmental data, structure it carefully, and translate it into outputs that inform decisions. That is what I understand the carbon accounting and reporting work at Jacobs to require.
